Simulate Launch

OK, finally we are ready to launch the rocket!

The equation of motion is

$$ m a = F_{\text{thrust}}(t)-F_{\text{drag}}\left(z, \dot{z}\right)-F_{G}(t,z) $$

which we (as usual) represent as a first order system

$$ \vec{u} = \vec{F}(t, \vec{u}) $$ where velocity is \(v=\dot{z}\), and \(\vec{u} = (z,v)^T\). Then the required first order differential system is $$ \frac{d}{dt} \vec{u} = \begin{pmatrix} v\\ \big(F_{\text{thrust}}(t)-F_{\text{drag}}\left(z, v\right)-F_{G}(t,z)\big) \big/ m \end{pmatrix} $$

This system can be integrated using scipy.odeint to return a two dimensional matrix containing height and velocity.

What should be the time range? There are two good options here:

  • up to a little beyond the burn time (here I use 200).
  • up to a little beyond the time the rocket reaches maximum altitude (this depends on the model).

To keep things simple, we will select option 1 and only simulate up to 200 seconds after launch.
