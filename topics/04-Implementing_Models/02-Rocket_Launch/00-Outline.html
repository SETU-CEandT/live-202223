Project Outline

Introduction

We have covered a number of core computational tasks using scipy, in particular root finding and integration of differential equations. In this assignment you are going to used these tasks to solve a sequence of models based around rocket launch. At the most basic level, the equation of motion for a rocket can be treated in the same way as the damped driven pendulum (that we covered in week 10) both are affected by three forces: a driving force, a damping drag force, and a gravitational force. In the case of rocket motion, these forces are represented by the rocket's thrust force, atmospheric and wave drags, and the rocket's weight.

The balance of forces acting on a rocket are

$$ F_{\text{net}} = F_{\text{thrust}} - F_{\text{drag}} - F_{G} $$

Hence, using Newton's second law, the equation of motion for a rocket (in one dimension \(z\) = height)

$$ m \frac{d^{2} z}{d t^{2}} = F_{\text{thrust}}(t)-F_{\text{drag}}\left(z, \dot{z}\right)-F_{G}(t,z) $$ where

  • \(F_{\text{thrust}}(t)\), the thrust force, depends on time, \(t\).
  • \(F_{\text{drag}}\left(z, \dot{z}\right)\), the atmospheric and wave drag force, depend on height, \(z\), and rate of change of height.
  • \(F_{G}(z, t)\), the gravitational force, depends on height and time.
  • \(m\) is the mass of the rocket (+fuel).
